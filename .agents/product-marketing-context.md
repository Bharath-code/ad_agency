# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-16*

## Product Overview

**One-liner:** PromptLens shows B2B teams why AI assistants recommend competitors instead of them, then gives the exact content and positioning fixes to win those buyer prompts.

**What it does:** PromptLens scans buyer-intent prompts across AI assistants, detects whether a company or its competitors are recommended, explains the reasons competitors win, and turns missed prompts into prioritized actions. It combines AI visibility monitoring with product marketing diagnostics.

**Product category:** AI search visibility monitoring, generative engine optimization, AI recommendation diagnostics, competitive positioning intelligence.

**Product type:** B2B SaaS with optional one-time audit service.

**Business model:** Free diagnostic scan, paid SaaS plans, agency plan, and paid manual audits.

## Target Audience

**Target companies:** B2B SaaS companies from bootstrapped to Series A, especially teams investing in SEO, content, category creation, or founder-led growth.

**Decision-makers:** Founders, CEOs, heads of growth, product marketers, content leads, SEO leads, fractional CMOs, and agency owners.

**Primary use case:** Understand which AI buyer prompts recommend competitors, why competitors win, and what to fix.

**Jobs to be done:**

- Find out if AI assistants recommend our product for high-intent buyer questions.
- Understand why competitors appear more often or are described more clearly.
- Decide what content, proof, messaging, or positioning to ship next.
- Prove whether fixes improve AI recommendation visibility over time.

**Use cases:**

- AI visibility audit.
- Competitor prompt battle analysis.
- Weekly AI recommendation monitoring.
- Content strategy prioritization.
- Agency client reporting.
- Founder-led teardown marketing.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** Companies do not know whether AI assistants recommend them when buyers ask high-intent questions, and they do not know why competitors win.

**Why alternatives fall short:**

- Generic AI visibility dashboards show scores but not what to do next.
- SEO suites bundle AI tracking but may not provide deep product marketing diagnosis.
- Manual ChatGPT checks are inconsistent, untracked, and not repeatable.
- Agencies can do manual audits but lack scalable software and history.

**What it costs them:** Lost discovery, missed buyer consideration, weaker category perception, wasted content effort, and competitor advantage in AI-mediated buying journeys.

**Emotional tension:** Founders fear becoming invisible in the new discovery layer while competitors become the default recommendations.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**

- Recommendation diagnostics, not only visibility tracking.
- Prompt-level competitor win/loss analysis.
- Evidence-backed actions tied to raw transcripts.
- Confidence and variance scoring.
- SaaS-specific positioning and content recommendations.
- Agency-ready reporting path.

**How we do it differently:** PromptLens treats AI answers as buyer recommendation events. It asks which prompts matter, who wins, why they win, and what the customer should change.

**Why that is better:** Buyers do not need another vanity score. They need decisions: what page to publish, what proof to add, what comparison to write, what message to clarify.

**Why customers choose us:** They want a practical diagnostic workflow that turns AI search losses into a prioritized action plan.

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| AI answers change too much to track | PromptLens measures patterns over repeated runs and shows confidence, variance, and raw evidence. |
| Can you really improve AI recommendations? | We do not promise control. We identify missing proof, unclear positioning, and content gaps that improve the odds of being recommended. |
| SEO tools will add this | SEO tools track visibility broadly; PromptLens focuses on why competitors win and what product/marketing action to take. |
| I can check ChatGPT myself | Manual checks are inconsistent, not tracked, not multi-model, and do not create weekly evidence or trend history. |

**Anti-persona:** Hobby projects, local businesses with no content strategy, companies that want guaranteed manipulation of AI answers, and teams unwilling to act on positioning/content recommendations.

## Switching Dynamics

**Push:** Founders see competitors mentioned in ChatGPT/Claude and have no way to diagnose or fix it.

**Pull:** PromptLens gives prompt-level evidence, competitor reasons, and concrete actions.

**Habit:** Teams rely on Google SEO tools and occasional manual AI checks.

**Anxiety:** Buyers worry AI answer behavior is too random, the category is too new, or recommendations will not produce ROI.
